Responsible Gaming

At Amazon Studio, we always want our players to have a fun and entertaining experience playing our games. We create great entertainment content in the industry and millions of players play our content around the world in a healthy way. While many of our games offer a social casino experience, none of our games offer the possibility of winning real money or prizes. All coins, chips and cards used in our games have no real monetary value. They are only suitable for use in our games and cannot be exchanged for real money or transferred or swapped between games, players or other accounts in the real world.

Amazonia Studio encourages all players to engage in responsible gameplay. Gameplay is responsible when players: Have a fun and entertaining experience. Can control the amount of time they spend playing games, their spending, and any purchases made in games. Find an appropriate balance between games and other life activities, including personal and family responsibilities and other leisure activities.

While most people have a fun, rewarding and relaxing experience playing video games, other gamers struggle to control their game or purchases made during game play. These gamers may be at risk for developing a video game behavior disorder. If you believe that you or a family member exhibits any of the following behaviors, you or your family member may have, or may be at risk for developing, a video game behavior disorder:

  • You have difficulty controlling the time you spend playing video games;
  • You have difficulty properly prioritizing video games with other activities;
  • You show interest in video games that negatively interfere with other activities, responsibilities, or obligations in life;
  • You increase your gaming or spending on video games, despite the increased negative consequences caused by such an action.

It is important to note that for a video game behavior disorder to be diagnosed, the pattern of behavior must be of sufficient severity, resulting in significant impairment of personal, family, social, educational, occupational, or other important areas of functioning, and usually must have been evidenced for at least 12 months.

If any of the above behaviors apply to you, consider conducting some research into the problem and, if necessary, seeking help from a qualified medical professional.
